Chapati Recipe

Below is a guide to prepare Kenyan chapati

From my stay in Kigali, Rwanda, it turned out that many people love the Kenyan chapati, so I decided to create a recipe to help those who'd love to prepare it themselves at home.

Ingridients

  • 2 cups wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Â¾ cup warm water, or as needed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Make the dough
    • Combine whole wheat flour, salt, and water in a large bowl to form a shaggy dough.
    • Knead on a floured surface until smooth and elastic. Adjust water or flour as needed.
    • Form into a ball, place in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out the chapati
    • Divide the dough into equal balls.
    • Roll each ball into a thin circle on a floured surface. Thinner dough makes fluffier chapati.
    • Brush with melted ghee for extra flavor if desired.
  3. Cook the chapati
    • Heat a griddle or tawa over medium heat.
    • Place the rolled dough on the hot pan, cooking until bubbles appear (30 seconds to 1 minute).
    • Flip and cook until light brown spots form (another 30 seconds to 1 minute). Press with a spatula to create puffs.
    • For a flakier chapati, add a bit of oil or ghee around the edges while cooking the second side.
  4. Serve and enjoy!
    • Transfer cooked chapati to a plate or roti basket, covering with a clean cloth to keep warm.
    • Serve hot with beef and potato stew.
